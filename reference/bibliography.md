# Reference

## Core Framework

**Brock, W.A. & Hommes, C.H. (1998).** "Heterogeneous beliefs and routes to chaos in a simple asset pricing model." *Journal of Economic Dynamics and Control*, 22(8-9), pp.1235-1274.

The foundational framework adapted for population flight dynamics. The key insight — that heterogeneous agents with different belief systems and switching thresholds create non-linear dynamics including herding cascades — maps directly to evacuation behavior where different population segments have different risk tolerances.

**Beaumont, M.A., Zhang, W. & Balding, D.J. (2002).** "Approximate Bayesian Computation in Population Genetics." *Genetics*, 162(4), pp.2025-2035.

The ABC methodology used for scenario probability estimation. The soft kernel variant used here (rather than hard rejection) follows subsequent refinements in the ABC literature.

## Data Sources

**UAE Ministry of Defence (2026).** Daily operational summaries published via @modgovae on X.com, February 28 – March 6, 2026.

Primary data source for attack volumes, interception rates, and weapon type categorization. Daily infographics provide cumulative and daily-new figures across ballistic missiles, cruise missiles, and drones.

**General Civil Aviation Authority (GCAA).** Airspace status and capacity recovery bulletins, March 2026.

Used for airport capacity constraint modeling in the population flight module. Recovery curve data inferred from operational bulletins and FlightRadar24 aggregated movements.

## Related Work

**Kirman, A. (1993).** "Ants, Rationality, and Recruitment." *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, 108(1), pp.137-156.

Early heterogeneous agent model demonstrating how simple behavioral rules produce complex aggregate dynamics. Influenced the social contagion mechanism in the population flight model.

**LeBaron, B. (2006).** "Agent-based Computational Finance." In *Handbook of Computational Economics*, Vol. 2, pp.1187-1233.

Comprehensive survey of agent-based models in financial markets, providing methodological foundations for the HAM stability analysis approach.
